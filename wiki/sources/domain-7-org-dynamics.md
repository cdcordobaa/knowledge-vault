> **TLDR:** Organizational Dynamics — AI reshapes teams, not just code. Dell'Acqua (P&G): Individual+AI matches Team performance. SPACE framework shows AI inflates Activity while degrading Communication. Junior developers gain speed but lose learning. Academia-industry gap: industry needs architecture AI, academia focuses on benchmarks.

## Key Claims

[FACT] Dell'Acqua's P&G experiment (n=776 professionals) demonstrates that Individual+AI achieves performance equivalent to Team without AI, suggesting AI can break organizational silos by giving individuals team-level cognitive resources. [src: dell-acqua-2025]

[FACT] The SPACE framework identifies 5 dimensions of developer productivity: Satisfaction, Performance, Activity, Communication, and Efficiency. AI tools measurably inflate Activity metrics while potentially degrading Communication and Satisfaction. [src: space-framework-2021]

[FACT] METR's 43-point perception-reality gap (19% slower, believed 24% faster) represents a direct organizational risk: leadership making headcount and process decisions based on developer self-reports will systematically overestimate AI productivity gains. [src: metr-2025]

[FACT] Junior developer deskilling is an emerging pattern: AI assistance accelerates task completion but bypasses the struggle-based learning that builds deep understanding, analogous to aviation's automation-induced skill atrophy. [src: dell-acqua-2025, dora-2018-2025]

[FACT] Zhang & Yu's systematic review reveals that industry's highest-priority need — AI for requirements engineering and architecture — has the least academic research coverage, while academia over-indexes on code generation benchmarks that industry considers solved. [src: zhang-yu-2025]

[FACT] DORA's 7 team archetypes show that AI adoption outcomes depend on pre-existing team culture: high-trust teams with strong practices see amplified performance, while low-trust teams see amplified dysfunction, creating a widening gap. [src: dora-2018-2025]

[FACT] Senior developer skill atrophy follows a predictable pattern: over-reliance on AI for routine tasks erodes the deep system knowledge that is critical for architecture decisions, incident response, and mentoring — the highest-value activities. [src: dell-acqua-2025]

## Cross-Cutting Themes

[INFERENCE] The combination of junior deskilling and senior atrophy creates a "hollowing out" risk: organizations may lose the ability to develop the next generation of senior engineers while simultaneously degrading the current generation's skills. [inferred from: dell-acqua-2025, dora-2018-2025]

[INFERENCE] The academia-industry misalignment identified by Zhang & Yu explains why AI coding tools remain stuck at function-level assistance despite years of research — the incentive structure of academic publishing favors benchmark-able tasks over the messy, context-heavy problems industry actually needs solved. [inferred from: zhang-yu-2025, space-framework-2021]
